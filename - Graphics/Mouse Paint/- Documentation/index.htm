MousePaint Pre-Script

MousePaint is a historic piece of software written by the genius, Bill Budge, who also wrote Pinball Construction Set. It is modeled after the Macintosh equivalent, MacPaint, by Bill Atkinson. The following page is not exactly a history; consult the rest of the internet for that. Rather, it is an excerpt from the Apple Mouse IIc User's Manual (pages 10-27), explaining the use of MousePaint.

Comments

Ignore any mention of "built-in drive" or "Apple IIc". The MousePaint software works on any Apple II (or emulator) with a mouse card (or mouse support).

The MousePaint section was reproduced within the confines of the HTML code, and will almost certainly not look like it does in the actual manual... but it was a noble attempt.

The original manual (see photo above) used a bold, condensed version of Univers for chapters, Helvetica for the section headings and copy, and a dot-matrix typeface for commands and menus.

MousePaint comes from the days when the Lisa and Macintosh desktops were still being roughed-out. Menu commands have changed since then. Items such as "Blank HiRes Screen" or "Put Back Picture" are rather cryptic by today's standards. It's also a little surprising and weird that there is no way to list files on a disk (or to know what disk is loaded) from within MousePaint. The absence of keyboard shortcuts for copy, paste, undo, and save sometimes make efficient painting awkward. Despite these minor flaws, MousePaint is a monument of 1980s computing.
